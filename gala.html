Although they serve quietly, not seeking recognition or accolades, the Green Berets take on the most dangerous
missions behind enemy lines to protect our families and our freedom. When these brave Special Forces
soldiers are traumatically wounded or killed in action, the Green Beret Foundation is there
to provide critical services and financial support for Green Beret families.

LIFETIME ACHIEVEMENT AWARD | Major General (R) Sidney Shachnow | Upholds Green Beret traditions and values

Surviving the Holocaust, coming to America and becoming the Commander of Det-A, Berlin Brigade, a clandestine unit of cold war Green Beret commandos on high alert 24-hours a day. This covert unit was made up of selectively trained and language qualified members of Special Forces, as well as many Eastern European immigrants who brought much needed culture, geographical and language skills to the assignment.

Major General (R) Sidney Shachnow is a Holocaust survivor who attained the rank of Major General in United States Army. After immigrating to America where he attended school for the first time in his life, he ultimately joined the US Army and in 1962 volunteered for the United States Army Special Forces, also known as the "Green Berets" where he served for the next thirty-two years until he retired in 1994.

Maj. Gen. Shachnow is the author of Hope and Honor, an autobiographical account of his childhood experience in the Nazi Kovno concentration camp of Lithuania, his immigration and assimilation to the United States and his 40 year career in the U.S. Army and Special Forces.

QUIET PROFESSIONAL AWARD | Joseph J. Grano, Jr.

As we look beyond the Post 9/11 global conflicts, Green Berets and their families need to look to the future and build a life away from constant war and selfless service. The character of a green beret will serve them well as they adapt to become productive business owners and integrate into corporate America.

Mr. Grano joined the Army, and after basic and advanced infantry training, went to officer candidate school and was trained to be a Green Beret officer. He was sent to Panama for two years and then posted to Vietnam as a captain. While serving in Vietnam as a company commander, he was severely wounded and retired with a 60% disability.

He is currently Chairman and CEO of Centurion Holdings LLC, a company that advises private and public companies. From 2001-2004, Mr. Grano was Chairman of UBS Financial Services Inc. In 2002, Mr. Grano was appointed by President George W. Bush to serve as the Chairman of the Homeland Security Advisory Council.

STEEL MAGNOLIA AWARD | Georgette Mosbacher | Spartan Female Force

The Steel Magnolias are an exclusive group of female caregivers, spouses and Gold Star wives of Army Special Forces Soldiers that have faced tragedy, challenges and adversity head on while continuing to demonstrate the warrior ethos and implement it into their daily lives to continue to support their families and their Green Berets. Those that demonstrate a high level of patriotism to our Country, the Green Beret Community and the Spartan female spirit are the only ones that gain membership into this elite and exclusive Green Beret group.

Ms. Georgette Mosbacher has demonstrated, throughout her life, a relentless sense of dedication and service to our country through her support of our military, the successes she has experienced in her career and being a truly outstanding role model for the ladies of the Special Forces Community.

Georgette Mosbacher is President and CEO of Borghese, a global cosmetic, skincare and fragrance company. She serves on two boards that support our troops and veterans; the Intrepid Museum Foundation and Intrepid Fallen Heroes Fund. She authored two books; Feminine Force a motivational guide for women who wish to achieve their potential, and It Takes Money Honey, a women's get smart guide to total financial freedom.
